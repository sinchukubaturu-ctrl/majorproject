About Glioma:

Gliomas are tumors that originate in the glial cells of the brain or spine. They are known for their potential to grow quickly and invade nearby tissue. Early detection and diagnosis are essential for treatment planning.

About Meningioma:

Meningiomas are tumors that arise from the meninges—the membranes that surround the brain and spinal cord. They are usually benign (non-cancerous) and slow-growing, but their location can cause symptoms as they compress brain tissue.

{% elif pred_label.lower() == 'pituitary tumor' %}
About Pituitary Tumor:

Pituitary tumors develop in the pituitary gland, a small gland at the base of the brain. They are often benign and can cause problems by either over- or under-producing hormones, or by pressing on nearby brain structures like the optic nerves.
